[Part 2] -- Prague Recap - Students Here

Posted on Sun 25 June 2017 in Prague Study Abroad

The Students at CTU

I can't say enough good things about the Czech students at CTU that I've met so far.

^ I wrote that above back in February, and I'd extend it to all of the Czech students that I met throughout the year. I met an incredible amount of international students too. I'm proud to call so many interesting and dynamic people my friend this semester, and I sincerely hope we cross paths again one day.

There was something interesting to do every day with all sorts of interesting people. I don't think it would do anyone justice to try to write about every one of them, because I don't want to write that much and I'd definitely leave something out. I guess the common factor was that we all had strong individual personalities and a lot of energy, but got along well.

Again, the people here were what made studying abroad great for me.

They tell me that a huge amount of young people in Prague speak English, but fewer of the older and rural people speak English.

I found out that a huge amount of people in Europe speak English. One time I was waiting for a bathroom at a train-station in rural Czech Republic, and a scruffy guy in overalls says something to me in Czech. I was spacing out, and asked him to say it again (in Czech), but then he stopped a moment and started again in English.

Anyways, Czech is a hard language to learn.

I still agree. Czech is a hard language for a native english speaker to learn. I've been more interested in learning in the past month since my classes have started to chill out. Czech grammar is pretty foreign, and I think its interesting. I found a few students that study full-time here, but don't know much Czech, so I think that its sort of 'optional' to learn.

Most of the bars/clubs have menus in English anyways, and respond to me in English when I try to open with "pivo prosim."

This is still true, even in Czech cities other than Prague. I really appreciate that its easy for English speakers to get around in the Czech Republic, but its also really satisfying to communicate something with someone in their native language. It's not too often you get to learn a foreign language in the country that its spoken in, so I'm going to keep at it until I go home.

Most people that I talk to seem more interested in American music, movies and politics than I am.

This trend continued throughout the year. Last week this guy from Italy started chatting me up about the Celtics, and man, he knew everything that happened since Larry Bird. Puts me to shame.

Yes, I did hear a lot of Trump comments. The Trump comments peaked during March and gradually tapered off throughout the year. I'm greatful, because I would rather talk about the mold on the ceiling than the latest tweet from the commander in chief.
