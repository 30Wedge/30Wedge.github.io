[Part 2] -- Prague Recap - Haiku moments

Posted on Fri 23 June 2017 in Prague Study Abroad

A Haiku moment

Haiku moment:

"an aesthestic moment' of a timeless feeling ~~of enlightened harmony as the poet's nature and the environment are unified~~" - Kenneth Yasuda (corny parts struck through)

So here are some of my haiku moments.

Pidgeons in Brussels

On a trip, I stayed in Brussels for two days and decided all my calories would come from chocolate, waffles and fries.

At the end I found myself with 2 extra hours before having to leave for the airport. I ordered one last waffle (my fifth of the day) and watched the sunset at the Jardin du Monts des Arts.

I noticed two pidgeons fighting over a waffle. This wasn't one of the chalky 1-Euro chalky waffles that, but one of the good 3-Euro gaufres. The waffle was elastic enough to keep the pidgeons from tearing it apart. But the two pidgeons kept at it until they struggled out of sight with the waffle.

I like to think that they're still trying to eat it today.

International Squad Goodbye Party

I got invited to a friend of a friend's goodbye party. My group was the first to show up, and we got chatting with the hosts. By the time I left there must have been 45 people in his appartment, and everyone was speaking English. I was the only native English speaker there.

On my way out I realized that when I left they weren't speaking English just for me, and I'm not sure why I would have thought otherwise.

Drunk patronizing the vending machine until it breakes

Spare change and alcohol were my worst enemy. In the hall between the dorm's lobby and my bed, there is a vending machine that sells chocolate bars. Normally, I just look at it.

But, coming home after a few beers, I always check my pockets for spare change to get a candy bar.

By May I had tried every European candy bar in the machine, but also in May, the machine stopped working.

It wouldn't take any coins, even if you feed them in three or four times. Probably to my benefit.

Just being content sitting still

Went to the Letna park to picnic with friends a few times. Was really just content sitting on the grass and talking about nothing. These were some of the best days.

German trains aren't on atomic time

On my way to Belgium I took a detour through Germany that involved maybe five train transfers. Two of the transfers involved 5 minute layovers. "Not a problem," I thought. German trains are never late.

One of the trains was 3 minutes late to one of those critical transfers. I spent 30 seconds at the station double checking which umlauted train name was the one I wanted,

then the next 30 seconds second guessing myself,

then the last 30 seconds speed walking towards the train I needed to get on as it left the station.

This actually didn't turn out to be a big deal because the info desk hooked me up with a bus to the next station I needed.

The second hairwidth transfer was also 3 minutes late. No indicision or second guessing this time; I learned that lesson.

I speedwalked all the way accross the platform. (Next time I'll briskly jog).

I got caught between the doors of my next train as they were closing. When stuck on something soft between them, the doors of German ICE trains close with a surprising ammount of force before opening again to let the soft object pull himself and his backpack onto the train.

Heroes of the Hydrich terror vs. Technogym

Technogym is an Italian fitness company that made some really un-ergonomic isolation training gear in the previous millennium. CUT's K.N. campus has an okay weightroom in the basement that could be good if they got rid of all the 80's Technogym junk that nobody uses anyways. I had an hour time slot ever week where I could go work out before Wenesday classes.

During Nazi occupation of Prague, Czech underground soldiers assasinate Reinhard Heydrich, #3 ranking Nazi, in Prague. The assasins were betrayed and eventually were forced to hole up in the crypt of a church as 800 Nazi soliders tried to force them out. The standoff ended after the Nazis started flooding the crypt with water and the assasins committed suicide.

On the map, these two basements are no more than 90m apart from eachother.
