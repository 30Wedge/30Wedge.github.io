Date: Sun June 25 2017
Category: Prague Study Abroad

##Eurasmus?

> From what I've heard about Eurasmus, is that the classes are significantly easier and the students' focus is about stretching your savings to cover 
> as much traveling and drinking as possible.

I wouldn't say that the classes are "_significantly easier_"(maybe "_less hard_" is a better term).
And yeah, traveling and social stuff (such as beer if one is in Prague) have a pretty high priority.

Eurasmus is about cultural exchange.
People from all over the world came together here to learn something about other cultures.
And having fun with each other turns out to be a great way to learn about other cultures.

Seems like a pretty good concept to me.


> That's mostly okay with me, but at the same time I'm trying to take some of the core heavy-lifting courses for my major. 
> I don't want to skimp out on the learning.


I'm so glad I got to take my core classes here. I don't think I missed enough academically to be concerned.
Even if I did skimp a little on the academics, I think I'll be fine going into my senior year with everything I learned here.

Plus, I've been lucky enough to have a 9-week internship over the summer, 
and I usually learn more usefull stuff from a summer internship than a year of school anyways.

> I'm moving forwards with a lot of energy and a balanced mindset.

^That's the spirit.

##Parting

It's been tough saying bye to everyone I've met over the semester. It seems like every day for the past three weeks a friend has left to travel or go home.
Pretty soon it will be only me and Tim staying for the internship over the summer.
Part of the fun of this semester was that it couldn't last forever.
So I came in already knowing that everything would end this summer, but I'm still bummed out a little bit.

We're moving accross town to an appartment in a few days, and we'll stay for the next eight weeks in Prague.
I feel lucky to have the internship, but it will definitely be a change of pace.
I think that come August, I'll be able to say goodbye to the city and go back home to get on to the next thing.

I hope that my path will cross again with some of the people that I've met in Prague.
