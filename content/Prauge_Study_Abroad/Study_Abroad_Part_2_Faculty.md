Title: [Part 2] -- Prague Recap - Faculty
Date: Sun June 25 2017
Category: Prague Study Abroad


##The Faculty at CTU

> I've only had a few interactions. The few prof's I met seemed to be relatively distant with no rush to be anywhere.
> The word **aloof** fits well.

I think that _aloof_ fits European academia _as a whole_ as opposed to the states, and not just for the professors.
School feels a lot less frantic here. 
For example, during one of my finals, the professor let me know how many points I needed to pass,
and encouraged me to stop once I answered enough questions so we could both go home early (I took his suggestion).

Maybe its because I just went to international classes, or maybe its because students really are just more chill here.
Maybe it has something to do with the price of college being a fraction of it is in the states.
If you fail a class here and need an extra semester, you just take it next year (and maybe pay a $500 tuition fee),
but if you fail a class in the U.S. and need an extra semester, you're paying an extra $5k+ in tuition to retake it. 
The stakes are high.


> Here, I have a seperate paper form keeping track of my student status, international student status, public transit, course record and rent payment.
> There's also two online accounts -- one for CUT and a seperate one for the Faculty of Electrical Engineering.
> If I want to change my thumbnail picture for either accounts, I can't just upload a picture.
> I have to visit _another_ office.
> Those are really just **trivial** complaints so far. I'm still culture-shocked

^Now I disagree with that bolded bit. I don't think those complaints are trivial now.
There's a good deal of B.S. bureaucracy to navigate and it takes more time and effort than I think should be spent on bureaucracy.
but, hey, I survived it so how much can I really complain?

> I appreciate that the school here keeps spending practical.
I feel like Czech culture is a lot more focused on practicality.
Why take your car to the garage when you can learn how to fix it yourself?

> I haven't seen any stupid $90M buildings here that don't have any classrooms.
I found one!
On the east side of campus there is a 9-story glass office building for the school.


> I haven't seen the labs yet.
> I don't think these will differ much.
I mostly liked the computer labs here.
Some ran Linux, others ran Windows.
They usually had a common password to log in.
One lab needed a hardware update pretty badly (Windows 7 sticker on the case and trying to run Windows 10).
Another lab had some very nice Zynq embedded development boards for the whole class.

> I also haven't seen any of the classrooms I'll have. It was a little surprising to me that there wasn't some kind of tour around the Electrical
> Engineering buildings, but thats ok. **I'll be happier figuring it out on my own anyways.**

^Preach. I had a ton of fun trying to figure out where to go.
On my first day of the semester, for my first class, I went to the room I thought I was supposed to go to.
And nobody was there.
I hung around for 15 minutes than tried to ask a cook at the cafeteria if I was at the right place.
Turns out my class was on a different campus 20 minutes away by metro, but the surprise was all part of the fun.
